About these notes

This site is my reading notebook. I jot down short, honest summaries of papers, ideas that resonate with me, and small sparks for future projects in computational pathology and multi-omics.

Most entries are lightweight: a few sentences on what the paper did, what I found useful, and how it might connect to nuclei segmentation, spatial transcriptomics, or multimodal reasoning.

About me

I'm Ye Zhang (张烨), working on nuclei instance segmentation, multi-omics & spatial integration, and multimodal foundation models for cancer research.

I enjoy building datasets, thinking about how histology reflects underlying omics, and exploring how models can support real clinical questions instead of only benchmarks.

If you're interested in similar topics or want to collaborate, feel free to reach out — this notebook is also my way of thinking out loud.
